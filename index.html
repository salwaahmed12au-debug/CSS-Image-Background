Karachi: The Economic and Cultural Heart of Pakistan

Karachi, the country's largest metropolis and its former capital, serves as the vital lifeline of Pakistan’s economy, contributing over half of the nation's federal revenue through its two major seaports and massive industrial sectors. Often called the "City of Lights," it is a sprawling, high-energy melting pot where diverse ethnic communities converge, creating a unique fusion of traditional South Asian heritage and modern urban life. From the historic colonial architecture of Saddar to the serene shores of the Arabian Sea, Karachi remains the ultimate hub for trade, fashion, and the arts, driving the country's financial and social pulse despite its rapid expansion and infrastructure challenges.
